Physics increasingly relies on Bayesian techniques for rigorous data analysis and modelto-data comparison. This paper describes how these methods can be implemented to answer
questions of relevance to the teaching of introductory physics concepts. It demonstrates
the proper application of statistical approaches to quantitatively assess when more complex
calculations are necessitated by data. The case study of a simple pendulum allows for
the incorporation of the precision commonly found in introductory physics laboratories in
order to rigorously assess model preference between the small angle and exact expressions
for the period. This extends the current simple pendulum literature’s focus on individual
assessments of different approximations to the exact solution to place such discussions on a
data-driven foundation and provides guidance to instructors for teaching laboratory design.
